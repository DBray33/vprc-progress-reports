# Bottom Line

<p>March was a month of two stories: the portfolio is quietly building real authority while surface-level traffic metrics took a hit. The biggest win is that <strong>estimated organic traffic value climbed to $35,675 (+79.8% MoM)</strong> and <strong>#1 rankings grew to 12 (+9.1%)</strong>, signaling that the keyword foundation is strengthening in the positions that matter most. The click and session declines are real and worth watching — <strong>clicks dropped to 237 (-35.1% MoM)</strong> and <strong>organic sessions fell to 337 (-35.8% MoM)</strong> — but these correlate with a position slip to <strong>23.9 average position (-2.8 positions MoM)</strong> and a high churn in the keyword portfolio, not a structural collapse. The priority for April is clear: recover the high-value keywords that dropped sharply (particularly "roof repair services" and the Bethpage/gutter cluster), publish spring seasonal content now while inspection intent is peaking, and tighten CTR on the 20 identified problem keywords sitting in high-impression, low-click limbo.<br>---</p>
