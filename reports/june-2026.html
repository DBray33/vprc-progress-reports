EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Leads look solid this month: 17 legitimate leads came in from 18 total submissions, with zero spam getting through, so that's a clean inbox and a decent pipeline for June. Clicks came in at 176, down 7.4% from last month, which is worth watching but not alarming. The bigger number, impressions, dropped 19.2%, but nearly all of that loss came from deep-position, never-clicked queries that weren't sending traffic anyway; rankings actually improved on over 500 of those queries at the same time, so the site is getting cleaner, not weaker. Organic sessions landed at 260, down 13.3%, and we're holding 27 keywords at position one, which is a real asset to protect and build on. On AI search, 28% of 612 customer-style queries tested across ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity mentioned Valley Peak, meaning roughly one in four times a homeowner asks an AI a roofing question in your area, your name comes up. Perplexity is the strongest at 34%, Gemini sits at 31%, and ChatGPT is the gap to close at 20%. For July, the priority is publishing a storm damage identification page targeting wind and rain damage on shingles, since summer storms are active and homeowners are searching right now. Second, shore up the ChatGPT gap by getting the business listed and reviewed on Yelp, Angi, and BBB, since that's where ChatGPT pulls local recommendations.
