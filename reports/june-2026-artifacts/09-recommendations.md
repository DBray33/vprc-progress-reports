# Strategic Recommendations

## 1. Fix the Homepage Duplicate Indexing and Title Tag Crisis Bleeding 40,000+ Impressions

**Problem:** The homepage is split across two separate indexed URLs, `http://www.valleypeakroof.com/` (20,596 impressions, CTR of 0.058%) and `https://valleypeakroof.com/` (20,222 impressions, CTR of 0.26%), together accounting for over 40,000 impressions with a combined click yield of only 65 clicks, while high-value keywords like "roofing companies" (2,524 impressions, position 1.4), "roofing contractor" (2,057 impressions, position 1.4), and "roof replacement contractors" (1,842 impressions, position 1.1) are ranking in the top 2 positions but generating zero recorded clicks.

**Action:** 

**Impact:** Consolidating the duplicate URLs should eliminate split signals and push the canonical page higher in rankings within 4 to 8 weeks. Even a modest CTR improvement from 0.26% to 1.5% on the combined 40,000+ impressions would yield roughly 600 additional clicks per month, directly feeding the lead pipeline.

**Priority:** High

## 2. Rescue the Gutter Page: 25,864 Impressions Producing Only 4 Clicks

**Problem:** The page `https://valleypeakroof.com/gutter-maintenance-installation` is the single largest impression source in the entire site at 25,864 impressions, yet it produced only 4 clicks for a CTR of 0.015%, and the problem keyword data shows "gutter repair" (7,448 impressions, position 44) and "gutter repair near me" (3,882 impressions, position 38) are both ranking too deep to earn clicks, while "gutter replacement" sits at position 53 with 2,451 impressions and zero clicks.

**Action:** 

**Impact:** Moving "gutter repair" from position 44 to the top 10 is a realistic 3 to 5 month target with dedicated, well-optimized pages. Given 7,448 impressions already exist for that single keyword, breaking into the top 10 at even a 3% CTR would generate over 220 additional clicks monthly from that term alone.

**Priority:** High

## 3. Publish Two July Storm Season Content Pieces to Capture Seasonal Demand

**Problem:** The site lost 2,014 keywords this month (net loss of 465 ranked keywords, down 11.5% MoM) while estimated organic traffic dropped 15.1% MoM to 2,439, and the content portfolio has no published assets targeting the high-intent summer storm damage queries that roofing homeowners are actively searching in July.

**Action:** 

**Impact:** Seasonal content targeting active search queries typically begins generating impressions within 2 to 4 weeks of indexing. These two articles directly address the keyword loss trend by adding new rankable assets and building topical authority that supports the existing service pages, with measurable traffic contribution expected within 60 days.

**Priority:** Medium

## 4. Improve CTR on the Roof Financing and Budget Cost Pages Before More Impressions Are Wasted

**Problem:** The page `https://valleypeakroof.com/roof-financing-options` received 1,626 impressions with only 1 click (CTR of 0.062%), and `https://valleypeakroof.com/budget-roof-cost` received 1,507 impressions with zero clicks and a CTR of 0%, representing nearly 3,200 impressions per month generating no traffic whatsoever.

**Action:** 

**Impact:** Title tag and meta description optimization typically produces CTR improvements within 2 to 4 weeks of Google recrawling the pages. Moving from 0% and 0.06% CTR to even 1% on these pages would convert roughly 32 additional clicks monthly from impressions that are currently completely wasted.

**Priority:** Medium

## 5. Leverage the 28% AI Visibility Rate by Strengthening the Content Signals That Drive Perplexity and Gemini Citations

**Problem:** Valley Peak Roofing achieved a 28% AI Visibility Rate (178 of 612 responses) with Perplexity leading at 34% and ChatGPT trailing at 20%, but 110 of 204 prompts received zero mentions on any platform, meaning more than half of all tested queries produce no brand presence in AI-generated answers, which is an increasing share of how homeowners research roofing contractors.

**Action:** 

**Impact:** AI visibility improvements are slower to materialize than traditional ranking changes, with meaningful movement typically visible over 60 to 90 days as models recrawl and reindex updated content. Closing the gap between the current 28% visibility rate and the 53% reach score (prompts where at least one platform mentioned the brand) is the near-term benchmark, targeting consistent multi-platform mentions on prompts where only one platform currently cites the brand.

**Priority:** Medium
