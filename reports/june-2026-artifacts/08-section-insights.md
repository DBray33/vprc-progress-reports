# Section Insights

## ga4

Sessions dropped <strong>-12.4% MoM to 671</strong>, but the more telling signal is that organic search holds a <strong>58.5% engagement rate</strong> against a sitewide average of just <strong>42%</strong>, meaning the visitors who matter most are still engaging well. The volume dip is the problem to solve; quality is holding.

## trafficSources

Direct traffic is carrying <strong>275 of 671 sessions</strong> but converting at a weak <strong>23.6% engagement rate</strong>, dragging down the sitewide average. Organic search (<strong>260 sessions, 58.5% engagement</strong>) and paid search (<strong>78 sessions, 59% engagement</strong>) are the two channels actually delivering engaged visitors, making organic growth the clearest lever to pull.

## topPages

The homepage dominates with <strong>100 clicks at position 1.6</strong>, but the real opportunity gap is <strong>/gutter-maintenance-installation</strong>, which sits at <strong>position 37 with 28,641 impressions</strong> and only 4 clicks. That page is being seen constantly and ignored; a content and on-page overhaul could unlock significant traffic without needing to build new authority.

## localPack

<strong>10 Local Pack wins</strong> are a meaningful foothold, especially given that "near me" and city-specific queries are the highest-converting traffic in roofing. The priority now is feeding those pack listings with consistent GBP activity, since Local Pack presence can compensate for the <strong>-11.5% drop in ranked keywords</strong> by capturing leads that never reach organic results.

## keywords

There are <strong>20 problem keywords</strong> with high impressions but low clicks, which signals that Google is already surfacing the site for relevant queries but the titles or meta descriptions are not compelling enough to earn the click. These 20 keywords are the fastest path to traffic gains because the ranking work is largely done; the fix is copywriting, not SEO from scratch.

## competitors

<strong>alankunsmanroofing.com</strong> is the sharpest competitive threat, sharing <strong>45 keywords</strong> with Valley Peak while ranking across <strong>88 total keywords</strong>. Notably, <strong>homeshieldpros.com</strong> has the widest footprint at <strong>125 total keywords</strong> with 33 overlapping, meaning they are winning on volume and breadth; closing that gap requires expanding into more service-plus-city pages.

## competitorsWorthNoting

The same four or five names keep appearing across the keyword overlap data, which is actually a good sign. This is a defined, stable local market, not one being disrupted by a well-funded outsider. Valley Peak is already competing directly with the top players, and with <strong>alankunsmanroofing.com</strong> identified as having SEO gaps in location-specific pages and content depth, there is a realistic and near-term opening to outrank them on the service-plus-city terms that drive booked jobs.
